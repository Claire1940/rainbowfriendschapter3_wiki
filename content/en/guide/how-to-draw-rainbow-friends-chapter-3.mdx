<Section icon={Target} title="How to Draw Rainbow Friends Chapter 3: Brown Guide">

Brown is one of the standout new characters in the latest update, capturing the attention of fans worldwide. This guide provides a clear breakdown of how to draw rainbow friends chapter 3's newest clay-inspired monster. Brown features a unique, heavy-set body texture that mimics modeling clay, giving him a distinct look compared to the fabric-textured monsters like Blue.

To capture Brown's unique personality, you must focus on his heavy proportions and his signature melancholic expression. Follow these steps to construct his form from scratch.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Construct the Torso and Head">
    Draw a large, rounded pear shape for the main body. Unlike Blue, Brown's head and torso merge seamlessly without a distinct neck. Keep the base of the pear wide to emphasize his heavy, grounded stance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add the Stubby Limbs">
    Attach short, cylindrical limbs to the lower sides and bottom of the torso. Brown's arms are relatively short and thick, ending in rounded, fingerless hands. His legs should be stubby, supporting his massive frame.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sketch the Facial Features">
    Position two large, asymmetrical circles near the top of the face. Give him drooping, sad eyelids to capture his signature melancholy expression. Draw a simple, slightly curved line for his mouth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Clay Textures and Details">
    Add small, curved crease lines along the joints and body to simulate a clay-like texture. These subtle details are crucial for making your drawing look authentic to the Chapter 3 design.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Coloring Brown requires a warm, earthy palette. You want to create depth by using contrasting shades of brown and beige to simulate the uneven surface of molded clay.

| Part | Base Color | Shading Color | Blending Tip |
| Body Skin | Medium Chocolate Brown | Dark Espresso Brown | Blend edges lightly with a tortillon |
| Underbelly | Light Beige or Tan | Warm Grey | Use soft gradients to show volume |
| Eyes | Stark White | Light Cool Grey | Leave a tiny white speck for highlights |
| Crease Lines | Dark Brown | Black | Use a fine-tip pen for sharp contrast |

<Callout type="tip" title="Clay Texture Secret">
To make Brown look like he is made of clay, avoid perfectly smooth gradients. Instead, use short, choppy shading strokes to mimic hand-molded clay surfaces and capture his true in-game aesthetic.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Color Theory and Shading in Dark Environments">

The atmosphere of Rainbow Friends Chapter 3 is dark, eerie, and often illuminated by cold, blue ambient light. To make your drawings look professional, you must understand how color theory affects warm-toned characters like Brown and Cyborg Orange when they are placed in cool, blue environments.

When warm colors (like brown or orange) are exposed to cool blue light, their shadows do not simply turn darker shades of brown or orange. Instead, the shadows absorb the blue ambient light, shifting toward cool, desaturated purples and deep blues. This technique creates a striking contrast that makes your artwork pop off the page.

| Character | Base Color | Ambient Light | Shadow Tint | Highlight Color |
| Brown | Chocolate Brown | Blue Night | Deep Indigo | Warm Pale Gold |
| Cyborg Orange | Bright Orange | Cyan Glow | Muted Plum | Pastel Yellow-Green |
| Pink | Pastel Pink | Dark Purple | Deep Violet | Soft Lavender |
| Blue | Royal Blue | Yellow Spotlight | Dark Navy | Pale Cyan |

Applying these color theory rules will instantly elevate your fanart from a simple flat drawing to a dramatic, atmospheric masterpiece. Practice blending these contrasting tones to achieve a realistic 3D effect.
