<Section icon={Shield} title="Core Survival Mechanics for Beginners">

Success in Rainbow Friends depends on your ability to balance objective completion with stealth. The most critical tool in your arsenal is the **Box**. While it provides a sanctuary from some monsters, it is not a "god mode" button. Understanding when to hide and when to run is the foundation of any **rainbow friends chapter 3 beginner guide**.

### The Art of Hiding
In the upcoming Pirate Ship map, corridors are expected to be narrower, making the box mechanic even more vital. However, monsters like Green can still catch you if you collide with them while boxed. You must create distance before deploying your hide.

| Action | Best Used When... | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprinting** | Crossing open decks or escaping Orange's path | **High** (Makes noise) |
| **Boxing** | Blue is nearby but hasn't reached you yet | **Medium** (Vulnerable to Green) |
| **Crouching** | Moving through vents or near Purple's zone | **Low** (Slow movement) |
| **Waiting** | Listening for footsteps behind corners | **Very Low** (Safe scouting) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Audio Awareness">
Play with headphones. Every monster in Rainbow Friends has a distinct sound profile. Blue has heavy footsteps, Green has squeaky shoes, and Orange has a mechanical alarm.
</Callout>
